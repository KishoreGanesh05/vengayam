**CHENNAI** — The Municipal Administration and Water Supply Department 
recently released a roadmap for providing clean water supply to Chennai 
round the clock. The document outlines plans to achieve a task long overdue 
for the city's residents.

While the general consensus is that the plan is much needed, a section of 
internet citizens is not very happy.

"Dirty, yellow-coloured water is the lifeline of Chennai people. The 
government can never take it away from them," said an X user based in 
Amsterdam, expressing concern over the growing disconnect between the 
people and the government.

"Stinky, inedible water is not just water, it is the way of life," argues 
another X post with Karl Marx as their display picture, for some reason.


This joins a growing list of government initiatives facing fierce resistance 
from concerned citizens, including the "culture-destroying" footpath 
encroachment removal drive and the recent installation of streetlights 
along Marina Beach, which critics say unfairly disturbs homeless residents 
who had grown accustomed to the ambience of complete darkness. At this 
rate, the state government might as well give employment to the youth 
enslaving them to corporates and call it development, says concerned citizens in total seriousness.
